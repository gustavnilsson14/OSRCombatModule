Combat

GM decides a range of 3~ tiles available for player characters, place minis on mat.

Round lifecycle

  1. Handle battlemat features
  2. Roll for initiative, and set combat intensity
  3. Take tactical action
  4. Planning movement and action

Turn lifecycle

  1. Handle battlemat featuress
  2. Move all combatants simultaneously.
  3. Assists and defends
  4. Ranged attack preparation
  5. Melee and Ranged attacks

Combat end

  1. Handle equipment damage
  2. Restore endurance, half if wounded, full otherwise

Escape

2d8 below distance to closest enemy + (rounds passed * 2)

Tactical actions

  • Aim (more damage)
  • Brace (more defense)
  • Equipment (use stuff)
  • Catch Breath (heal)
  • Initiative
  • Escape
  • Boost (power up spells)
  • Cast Spell
  • Kidnap

Actions

  • Melee attack (each turn)
  • Ranged attack (every odd turn)
  • Assist (buff ally)
  • Defend (suffer only highest damage die)

Movement

  • Stand ground
  • Move to tile, move every other turn
  • Retreat, move back every turn
  • Charge, move forward every turn

Critical damage

Add another of the highest die rolled for damage and add to the attack.

Wounds and Injury

  • When wounded, max endurance is halved

Random an injury if;

  • Falling unconscious in combat
  • Taking injury out of combat

Initiative

When rolling, if below threshold, initiative goes to players, otherwise to enemies

Turn amount

The side which wins initiative decides to keep the turn amount or to increase, or decrease it by 1

Turn amount starts at 3, if it drops to 0, the combat is over and the side which has initiative escapes

Damage types

  • Cut
  • Stab
  • Pain
  • Gun
  • Burst
  • Blast
  • Ooze
  • Nuke
  • Light
  • Neural
  • Volt

Gameplay tips!

When solving a challenge;

  • Die rolls mean danger!
  • Be descriptive and specific
  • Use your inventory creatively
  • Your background matters

When in combat;

  • Death is never sudden
  • Exploit weaknesses
  • Use your inventory creatively
  • Sometimes you will need to escape
